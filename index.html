02

My blueprint for scaling B2B lead gen on Meta

  1. Validate on a hypothesis, not a vibe. Every test starts with a specific belief about why an angle will work, run at just enough spend to reach a real read, before it earns more budget.
  2. Prove the hook before you build the funnel around it. A creative concept earns a dedicated landing page and retargeting sequence only after it's proven at small spend, not before.
  3. Split always-on from tactical. One steady baseline campaign that never turns off, so the pipeline and the data both stay continuous, plus separate tactical campaigns for specific offers or timing.
  4. The guardrail metric is cost per qualified lead, never CPM or CTR. Scale by adding budget to what's already proven to convert, not by launching more new campaigns to chase volume.
  5. Kill fast, scale slow. Cut non-performers inside a defined test window. Scale winners in 20 to 30 percent steps so Meta's delivery system doesn't reset into a fresh learning phase.

Unprompted: how First Page Digital is running Meta right now

Pulled directly from Meta's public Ad Library against the "First Page Digital - Singapore" page, not a guess.

What's currently live: ~5 active ads, 2 creative concepts
Concept A — top of funnel: a podcast/webinar invite ("Every agency is selling a different AI Search framework... prepare your toughest questions") plus an AI Search Playbook download. Both point to podcast.firstpagedigital.sg.
Concept B — bottom of funnel: a single performance-guarantee ad, "40% Better Results Or We Work For Free," running with the identical headline and body copy across every format variant (static image and video). No second hook is being tested against it.

What I'd change in week one: right now these read as two disconnected single-creative campaigns rather than one system. There's nothing observable tying the podcast/playbook audience into the guarantee-offer retargeting funnel, and the guarantee ad has zero hook variation running against it, so there's no way to know yet whether "40% Better Results" is actually the strongest angle or just the only one in market. I'd build the bridge between the two (content audience → guarantee retargeting) and get 3 to 4 alternate hooks into rotation against the guarantee offer before touching budget, exactly the validate-before-you-scale approach above.

This required no account access, just the same due diligence I'd apply to any account before touching it. Happy to go deeper once inside the actual account and creative library.
